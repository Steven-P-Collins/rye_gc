Community Projects

Over the years The Rye Garden Club has been responsible for planting and maintaining a few gardens around Rye.

Every spring and fall we clean up, trim back and put the gardens to bed at the Square House and the Rye Library. We also have planted and maintained the Knapp House island, which is located on Milton Road adjacent to the Knapp House. Along with these gardens, we also plant the pots along Purchase Street. We plant the pots seasonally, usually four times a year. For the holidays, we decorate the Rye Library, make wreaths for various town buildings and grow amaryllis bulbs that we deliver to civic organizations around town.

Square House Project

In August 2008 the RGC revamped the foundation plantings and stone walkways of the Square House as part of their ongoing commitment to the beautification of this historic property. The RGC drew on its own talent and advice from the Rye Landmarks Committee. The look is unified, dignified and appropriate to a historic building.

Purchase Street Pots

In October, planters along Purchase Street were moved to better locations and were repotted with ilix and pansies for fall.

Rye Nature Center Preservation

RGC members helped to remove garlic mustard and Japanese honeysuckle from the Rye Nature Center.

Municipal Buildings Wreathes

Each winter the RGC designs and makes wreathes to be hung from the front of the Municpal Building. Members are seen here with our handmade bows for the wreathes.

Meals on Wheels

Rye Garden Club gathers a few times a year to make flower arrangements for Meals on Wheels trays.

Amaryllis Christmas Delivery

The RGC horticultural committee pots up and gives away beautiful, blooming amaryllis to our civic organizations - the library, City Hall, the fire station.

Deck the Halls Civic Day

Members of the Little Garden Club and RGC gathered over two days to string garland, hang wreaths, decorating trees and make the season bright at the library and downtown. RGC also decorates the eighteen planters and fills over 18 pots downtown four times a year with seasonal Christmas greens.
